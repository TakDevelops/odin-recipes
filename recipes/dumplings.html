Classic Pork Chive Dumplings

Grandma Lee's Pork Chive Dumpling Recipe

To me, dumplings are little balls of love. My Nai Nai always made them when I was growing up. Every Chinese New Year, my uncle and I would have dumpling eating competitions to see who can eat the most number of dumplings. Dumpling wrapping was also a family bonding activity! This recipe is straight from my Nai Nai's heart, made with love.

Ingredients

  • 1 packet dumpling pastry, about 24 pieces (available at Asian store)
  • 220g mince pork
  • 135g Chinese chives（韭菜)
  • 1 Tbsp light soy sauce
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 2 tsp cornflour
  • 1/2 tsp chicken powder (chicken bouillon powder)

Steps

  1. Rinse chives and pat dry with kitchen papers. Chop into small pieces.
  2. Mix mince pork with seasonings well and combine with chives thoroughly. Chill in refrigerator for 30 minutes.
  3. Prepare a little bowl of water for sealing dumplings. Place a heaped teaspoon of filling into the center of the dumpling pastry.
  4. Damp the edge with water and seal it tightly. (View the video below to see how to pleat a dumpling.)
  5. Heat up a bit of oil in a non-stick frying-pan. Place dumplings in a single layer without touching with each other. Fry them in batches until the bottom is light brown. Add 1/4 cup of boiling water, cover with a lid and cook until the water dries up. Dish up and serve with soy sauce, chili sauce or vinegar.
